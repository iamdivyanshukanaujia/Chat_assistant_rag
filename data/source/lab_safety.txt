🧪 UNIVERSITY LAB SAFETY GUIDELINES (RAG-FRIENDLY FORMAT)

Academic Year: 2025–2026
Purpose: To promote safe working practices in all engineering and science laboratories.
Principle: Safety first — no experiment may be performed without supervision and proper protective equipment.

🔰 GENERAL LAB SAFETY RULES

Lab coats, closed shoes, and ID cards must be worn at all times.

Eating, drinking, and chewing gum inside laboratories is prohibited.

Mobile phones must remain on silent and may only be used with permission.

Follow all instructions given by laboratory instructors and staff.

Report unsafe behavior, spills, or damaged equipment immediately to the lab assistant.

Know the location of emergency exits, fire extinguishers, and first-aid kits.

Do not conduct experiments without explicit permission.

Running, horseplay, and loud talking are strictly forbidden.

⚙️ MECHANICAL ENGINEERING LAB SAFETY
Hazards

Rotating machinery

High-temperature surfaces

Sharp tools

Compressed gas cylinders

Rules

Tie long hair and avoid loose clothing near machines.

Do not operate machines without supervision.

Ensure machine guards and shields are in place.

Use tongs or insulated gloves to handle hot materials.

Remove metal chips with brushes, not bare hands.

Emergency Procedures

Use emergency stop button to shut off machines.

Use a fire blanket for grease fires.

Report all injuries immediately.

💻 COMPUTER LAB SAFETY
Hazards

Loose wires and tripping risks

Overheating computers

Static discharge

Cable clutter

Rules

Do not connect unauthorized USB drives.

Installation of software is not allowed without lab permission.

No food or drinks near computers.

Save work frequently and properly log out.

Use ESD wrist straps when working with hardware components.

Cyber Safety Rules

Do not access restricted servers or private networks.

Use only licensed software.

Follow university network usage policies.

🤖 AI & DATA SCIENCE LAB SAFETY
Hazards

High-power GPU systems

Overheating compute servers

Electrical overloads

Sensitive data environments

Rules

Ensure proper ventilation for GPU units.

Never modify server hardware.

Handle datasets securely and follow privacy regulations.

Avoid plugging multiple high-wattage devices into a single extension board.

Data Ethics Guidelines

Use datasets only for approved academic work.

Do not store sensitive personal data without authorization.

Follow fairness, transparency, and responsible AI principles.

🔌 ELECTRONICS LAB SAFETY
Hazards

Hot soldering irons

High-voltage circuits

Capacitor discharge

Static-sensitive ICs

Rules

Always turn off power before adjusting wiring.

Use fume extractors when soldering.

Wear ESD straps while handling chips.

Discharge capacitors before touching circuits.

Do not work on live circuits without supervision.

Emergency Procedures

Turn off main power supply in case of short circuit.

Use CO₂ fire extinguishers for electrical fires.

⚗️ CHEMISTRY LAB SAFETY
Hazards

Corrosive chemicals

Flammable substances

Toxic fumes

Glassware breakage

Rules

Always wear gloves and goggles when handling chemicals.

Work inside a fume hood for volatile chemicals.

Label all reagent bottles clearly.

Dispose chemical waste only in designated containers.

Never pipette chemicals by mouth—use pipette fillers.

First Aid Procedures

For chemical spills on skin: rinse under water for 15 minutes.

For chemical eye exposure: use eyewash station immediately.

All accidents must be reported to the instructor.

🔭 PHYSICS LAB SAFETY
Hazards

Laser beams

High-voltage power supplies

Optical equipment

Cryogenic materials

Rules

Never look directly into lasers or reflections.

Secure optical components to prevent falls.

Handle cryogens with insulated gloves.

Follow voltage safety guidelines strictly.

🤖 ROBOTICS & IOT LAB SAFETY
Hazards

Moving robotic arms

High-current motors

Battery explosions

Sharp mechanical edges

Rules

Maintain a safe distance from robotic mechanisms.

Turn off power before wiring microcontrollers.

Do not overcharge lithium-ion batteries.

Check motor driver ratings before use.

Equipment Guidelines

Proper grounding of robotics workstations is mandatory.

Emergency stop buttons must be accessible.

Secure all loose wires to avoid entanglement.

🔧 WORKSHOP & MANUFACTURING SAFETY
Hazards

Grinding sparks

Welding arc flashes

Rotating machinery

Cutting tools

Rules

Wear welding masks during welding operations.

Safety shoes and gloves required at all times.

Never leave machines running unattended.

Clamp workpieces properly before machining.

Equipment Use

Only use angle grinders with protective guards.

Wear ear protection near noisy machines.

Report malfunctioning equipment immediately.

⚠️ HAZARD CLASSIFICATION

Low Risk: Computer Labs, AI Labs (non-hardware tasks)

Medium Risk: Electronics Lab, Physics Lab

High Risk: Mechanical Lab, Workshop, Chemical Lab, Robotics Lab
