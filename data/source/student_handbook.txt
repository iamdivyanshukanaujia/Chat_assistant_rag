Academic Year: 2025–2026
This handbook outlines academic rules, attendance requirements, exam regulations, conduct expectations, digital behavior, anti-ragging laws, disciplinary actions, and student support policies.

🎓 ATTENDANCE POLICY

Minimum attendance requirement: 75% in each course.

Attendance shortage rules:

65% to 75%: Allowed only with medical certificate or approved leave.

Below 65%: Student is not allowed to appear in exams under any circumstances.

Medical leave procedure:

Submit a medical certificate within 7 days of returning to campus.

Upload through the Academic Portal.

Approval from academic committee required.

Attendance notes:

Lab and theory attendance are calculated separately.

Students entering more than 10 minutes late are marked absent.

Attendance is updated weekly.

📝 GRADING SYSTEM
Grade Scale

O: Outstanding (90–100)

A+: Excellent (80–89)

A: Very Good (70–79)

B+: Good (60–69)

B: Above Average (50–59)

C: Average (40–49)

F: Fail (<40)

I: Incomplete

W: Withdrawn

SGPA / CGPA Rules

SGPA = Σ(Credit × GradePoint) / Σ(Credits)

CGPA = Weighted average of all SGPAs.

Improvement policy: Only F-grade students can repeat a course.

🧾 EXAM RULES AND REGULATIONS

University ID card is mandatory during exams.

Allowed items: pens, pencils, erasers, transparent bottle, non-programmable calculator.

Prohibited items: mobile phones, smart watches, Bluetooth devices, unauthorized notes.

Late entry rules:

Up to 15 minutes: entry allowed.

After 15 minutes: not permitted.

Malpractice policy:

First offense → Semester suspension + zero marks.

Repeat offense → Permanent debarment.

Answer Sheet Rules

Rough work only on last pages.

Do not tear pages.

Write ID clearly on top.

Exam Revaluation

Window: 14 days after results.

Fee: ₹1000 per subject.

Marks can increase, decrease, or remain same.

📚 ACADEMIC INTEGRITY
Plagiarism Policy

Allowed similarity: Below 15%.

Tools used: Turnitin, URKUND.

Actions:

15–30% → Rewrite with penalty.

30% → Zero marks + disciplinary notice.

Repeated cases → Semester suspension.

Collaboration Rules

Group assignments: only within assigned groups.

Individual work must not be shared.

AI-generated content must be cited.
